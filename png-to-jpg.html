💡 Why PNG to JPG?

📦Smaller file size — JPG files are typically 5-10x smaller than PNG for photos.
📧Email friendly — Smaller JPG files attach faster and don't hit size limits.
🌐Faster loading — JPG images load much faster on websites.
⚠️Transparency lost — JPG doesn't support transparency. Set a background color to replace it.

🎨 Quality Guide

💎95-100% — Near lossless. Best for printing or archiving.
✅80-92% — Excellent quality. Good balance for most uses.
🌐60-75% — Good for web images where file size matters.
📱40-55% — Smaller files for thumbnails and previews.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions – PNG to JPG

Does converting PNG to JPG reduce quality?
JPG uses lossy compression, which means some quality is lost during conversion. At 90-95% quality setting, the difference is barely visible to the naked eye for most images. At lower quality (60-75%), the file size reduction is significant with visible but acceptable quality loss. For logos, text, or images with sharp edges, consider keeping PNG format as JPG compression creates artifacts on such content.
What happens to transparency when converting PNG to JPG?
JPG does not support transparency. All transparent areas in your PNG will be replaced with a solid background color. By default this is white, but you can choose any color using the background color option — black, grey, or any custom color. If your PNG has no transparency, the conversion is straightforward with no background needed.
How much smaller will the JPG file be?
The size reduction depends on the image content and quality setting. For photographs and complex images, JPG at 90% quality typically produces files 3-8x smaller than PNG. For simpler images or those with large flat-color areas, the difference may be smaller. The stats panel shows total converted file size for comparison.
Can I convert multiple PNG files at once?
Yes. Drop multiple PNG files into the drop zone or use the file picker to select multiple files at once (up to 20 files). All files are converted with the same quality and background settings. Each converted JPG has its own download button, or you can download all at once.
Are my images uploaded to a server?
No. All conversion happens entirely in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Your images never leave your device, making this tool 100% private. It also works offline after the page loads.
