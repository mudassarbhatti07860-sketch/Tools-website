📖 Slug Format Guide

🔗kebab-case — Best for URLs. my-post-title
🐍snake_case — Python, some CMS. my_post_title
🐪camelCase — JS APIs. myPostTitle
🏗️PascalCase — Class names. MyPostTitle
🔵dot.case — Config keys. my.post.title

💡 SEO Slug Tips

✅Use hyphens — Google recommends kebab-case for URLs.
✅Keep slugs 3-5 words, under 60 characters.
✅Include your main keyword in the slug.
✅Always use lowercase to avoid duplicates.
❌Never use spaces or special chars in URLs.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions – Text to Slug Generator

What is a URL slug and why does it matter for SEO?
A URL slug is the part of a URL that identifies a specific page — e.g. in "https://example.com/blog/how-to-build-a-website", the slug is "how-to-build-a-website". Slugs matter for SEO because search engines use them to understand page content. A keyword-rich slug can improve rankings and make URLs more shareable.
Should I use hyphens or underscores in URL slugs?
Google officially recommends hyphens (kebab-case) to separate words in URLs. Google treats hyphens as word separators, so "how-to-build" is read as three separate words. Underscores are treated as connectors — "how_to_build" may be read as one word. For best SEO, always use hyphen-separated slugs for blog posts and pages.
What are stop words and should I remove them?
Stop words are common words like "a", "the", "and", "of", "in" that don't add much meaning to a URL. Removing them makes slugs shorter — e.g. "how-to-build-a-website-in-2026" becomes "build-website-2026". Shorter slugs are easier to type and share. Enable the "Remove stop words" toggle to try it out.
How does Bulk Mode work?
Bulk Mode lets you enter multiple titles — one per line — and generates slugs for all of them at once. This is useful when setting up a new blog, migrating content, or creating URL structures for multiple pages. The output shows all slugs in the same order as your input. You can copy all slugs or download them as a .txt file.
What is the ideal slug length?
SEO best practices recommend keeping slugs between 3-5 words or under 60 characters. Shorter URLs are easier to remember, share and type. Very long slugs can look spammy and may get truncated in search results. Use the Max Length option to automatically truncate slugs, and check the SEO score section to see if your slug meets best practices.
