❓ FAQ
Meta tags are HTML snippets placed in the <head> section of your page. They tell search engines and social platforms what your page is about, what to display in search results, and how to preview it when shared on social media. Proper meta tags are essential for SEO and social sharing performance.
Google displays around 50–60 characters of the title tag before truncating it. Keeping your title within this range ensures it displays fully in search results. The tool shows a real-time character counter and color-coded warnings to help you stay within limits.
Open Graph (OG) tags control how your page appears when shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Discord and many other platforms. Without OG tags, these platforms pick random content from your page, often resulting in poor-looking previews.
No. Google officially ignores the keywords meta tag and has done so since 2009. However, it is still used by Bing and Yahoo to a small degree. It is safe to include but do not rely on it as an SEO strategy.
The recommended Open Graph image size is 1200×630 pixels. This works well across Facebook, LinkedIn and WhatsApp. For Twitter summary_large_image cards, use 1200×628. Always use HTTPS URLs and keep images under 8MB.
